Beginner's Lab: Build Your First Circuit

Back to Lab

It is time to put theory into practice. In this lab, you will combine your knowledge of breadboards, resistors, and LEDs to build a complete, functioning circuit. The goal is to light an LED without burning it out.

Step-by-Step Construction

1. Gather Your Components

  • Power Source: A 9V Battery and a battery clip with red (+) and black (-) wires.
  • The Load: One standard LED (any color).
  • The Protection: One Resistor (ideally between 330Ω and 1kΩ) to limit the current.
  • The Canvas: Your solderless breadboard and a few jumper wires.

2. Place the Components on the Breadboard

  • Insert one leg of the resistor into a numbered column (e.g., column 10). Insert the other leg into a different column (e.g., column 15).
  • Take your LED and identify the longer leg (Anode +). Insert the Anode into the exact same column as the second leg of your resistor (column 15).
  • Insert the shorter leg of the LED (Cathode -) into a new, empty column (e.g., column 18).

3. Close the Loop with Power

  • Connect the Red wire (+) from your battery to the first leg of your resistor (column 10). This allows current to flow into the resistor, which will slow it down before it hits the LED.
  • Connect the Black wire (-) from your battery to the Cathode of your LED (column 18). This gives the current a path back to ground, completing the circuit.
  • If everything is wired correctly, your LED will instantly light up!
Troubleshooting Tip: If your LED doesn't light up, disconnect the battery immediately! Check your LED's polarity first (it only lets current flow in one direction). Then, use your multimeter on the Continuity setting to ensure your jumper wires aren't broken internally.
