Master Component Library

A quick-reference guide to the most common electronic components used in laboratory environments.

1. Resistors (Wattage & Packages)

Type / Material Typical Wattage Key Characteristics & Lab Usage
Carbon Film (Through-Hole) 1/4 W (0.25W) The standard beige resistors used in almost all breadboard projects. Usually 4-band color code. Inexpensive and perfect for basic current limiting.
Metal Film (Through-Hole) 1/4 W or 1/2 W Typically blue in color. Used when higher precision (1% tolerance) is needed. Usually features a 5-band color code.
Wirewound (Power) 1W to 10W+ Large, rectangular ceramic blocks. Used in power supplies or motor circuits where large amounts of heat need to be dissipated safely.
SMD (Surface Mount) 1/10 W to 1/8 W Tiny rectangular chips (e.g., 0805, 0603 packages) soldered directly to PCBs. Printed with a 3 or 4 digit numerical code instead of color bands.

2. Capacitors (Types & Polarity)

Capacitor Type Polarity Typical Range & Application
Ceramic Disc Non-Polarized Picofarad (pF) to Nanofarad (nF). Tiny orange/brown discs. Used for high-frequency noise filtering and decoupling near ICs.
Electrolytic (Aluminum) Polarized (+/-) Microfarad (µF) to Millifarad (mF). Cylindrical cans. Used for bulk power smoothing and low-frequency filtering. Explodes if wired backwards!
Tantalum Polarized (+/-) Nanofarad (nF) to Microfarad (µF). Small, yellow bead-like drops. Highly stable capacitance, used in precise timing and audio circuits.
Polyester Film (Mylar) Non-Polarized Nanofarad (nF) to Microfarad (µF). Green or red rectangular "chiclets". Great for audio coupling and handling higher voltages.

3. Transistors & MOSFETs (Active Components)

Part Number Description & Characteristics
General Purpose BJT (NPN) - Small-signal amplification and switching
BC547The most common NPN transistor for beginners in India/labs.
2N2222Famous high-speed switching NPN transistor in a metal or plastic (PN2222) case.
2N3904Standard North American general-purpose NPN.
BC548Lower voltage version of BC547, often used in audio pre-amps.
BC549Low-noise version of BC547, great for sensitive audio circuits.
General Purpose BJT (PNP) - Complementary pairs to the NPNs above; high-side switching
BC557The PNP equivalent of the BC547.
2N3906The PNP equivalent of the 2N3904.
2N2907The PNP equivalent of the 2N2222.
BC558Standard PNP for low-power signal processing.
Medium & High Power BJTs - Driving motors, solenoids, or high-power LEDs

BD140PNP complement to BD139.

TIP32C3A PNP complement to TIP31C.
