Technical Specifications

  • Resistance Value: Measured in Ohms (Ω), it indicates how much the resistor opposes current flow. Common values include 220Ω, 1kΩ, 10kΩ, etc.
  • Power Rating: Indicates how much power the resistor can safely dissipate without damage. Common ratings are 1/4W, 1/2W, and 1W. Often can be found by inspecting the physical size.
  • Tolerance: Represents the accuracy of the resistor's resistance value. Common tolerances are ±5% (gold band) and ±10% (silver band).

Decoding the Bands: 4-Band vs. 5-Band

Carbon film and metal film resistors use color bands to indicate their resistance value. Precision circuits require 5 bands, while standard breadboard circuits typically use 4 bands.

4-Band Resistors (Standard Precision)

  • Bands 1 & 2: The first two significant digits.
  • Band 3: The Multiplier (number of zeros to add).
  • Band 4: The Tolerance (Gold = ±5%, Silver = ±10%).

5-Band Resistors (High Precision)

  • Bands 1, 2, & 3: The first three significant digits.
  • Band 4: The Multiplier.
  • Band 5: The Tolerance (Usually Brown = ±1% or Red = ±2%).
